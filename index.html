About Me

My name is Nurudeen, a computer enginering masters student at the University of Texas at Dallas.
I was a peer educator for 2-years, allowing me to grow in my leadership skills and professional knowledge. As I mentored peers, I had to demonstrate mastery of core engineering concepts while facilitating a structured environment conducive for learning.
I was also a research lab manager and research assistant, roles that allowed me to work underneath a faculty member with expertise in Radio Frequency Identification (RFID). These roles sharpened my knowledge of RFID even as they prepared me to work collaboratively with others in a professional context.
I was also a Computer Aided Design (CAD) Intern at Beumer. This was a transformative experience as a it gave me a chance to explore the inner workings of manufacturing and product lifecycle. Finally, over the course of the last few years, I have had various leadership roles in student organizations. Throughout my undergrad career, I have ventured out of my comfort zone, leading to impactful research and internship experiences. I have also sought professionalization opportunities in preparation for the next chapter in my career. The hallmark of all of these experiences is the opportunity they afforded me grow as a self-starter, critical thinker, and engaged team member.
On the road to becoming an engineer I have completed a number of projects in hardware and software development utilizing skills like
• Embedded Systems Programming
• Web Development
• PCB Design
• SPICE and Antenna Simulation
• 3D CAD
